Granddad sends out Haiti, a squad known for their pace and directness. The Veteran will likely deploy a formation that maximizes their counter-attacking threat, perhaps a 4-3-3 or 4-4-2, looking to exploit any defensive vulnerabilities. Key to Granddad's strategy will be the electric pace of Frantzdy Pierrot up front, whose ability to hold up play and get in behind will be crucial. Midfielder Duckens Nazon will be tasked with linking play and providing creative sparks, while the defensive solidity of Ricardo Adé will anchor the backline.

The one tactical question that decides it for Granddad: Can his Haiti squad absorb the pressure and then unleash their rapid transitions effectively, turning defense into attack with devastating efficiency?

Warner answers with Scotland, a formidable unit built on grit, organization, and a potent midfield engine. The Young Phenom will likely opt for a disciplined structure, possibly a 3-4-3 or 4-2-3-1, to control the midfield battle and push forward with purpose. Warner's assets will rely heavily on the leadership and passing range of Scott McTominay in the engine room, dictating the tempo and breaking lines. The attacking prowess of John McGinn will be vital for driving forward and creating chances, while Andrew Robertson's relentless runs down the flank will be a constant threat.

The one tactical question that decides it for Warner: Can his Scotland squad maintain their defensive shape against Haiti's speed, while simultaneously imposing their physical and technical dominance in midfield to create consistent attacking opportunities?
