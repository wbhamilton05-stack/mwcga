🔬 THE SCOUTING REPORT

Barnes sends out Bosnia-Herzegovina, a squad known for its resilient defense and the undeniable quality of their veteran striker, Edin Džeko. Expect Barnes to instruct his Bosnian assets to play a compact 4-4-2 or 4-2-3-1, looking to absorb pressure and hit Warner's Qatar on the counter. The midfield battle, likely spearheaded by the likes of Miralem Pjanić, will be crucial in disrupting Warner's rhythm. The key tactical question for Barnes: Can he get enough service to Džeko and utilize wing play from assets like Miroslav Stevanović to truly threaten Qatar's goal, or will his squad be stifled by Warner's disciplined defensive lines?

Warner answers with Qatar, a team that has embraced a more fluid, possession-based approach under his shrewd guidance. With talents like Akram Afif and Almoez Ali providing pace and clinical finishing up front, Warner's Qatari assets will likely operate in a 3-5-2 or 4-3-3 formation, aiming to dominate possession and exploit any defensive gaps. Their ability to switch play and break lines will be paramount. The key tactical question for Warner: Can his Qatari squad's intricate passing and movement unlock Bosnia-Herzegovina's sturdy defense, or will they become frustrated and predictable against Barnes's organized structure?
