Barnes's Belgian assets often operate in a 3-4-2-1 or 4-3-3, aiming to control the midfield and exploit wide areas through wing-backs like Yannick Carrasco or Jeremy Doku. The tactical question for Barnes here is whether his defense, marshaled by Jan Vertonghen and Toby Alderweireld, can withstand Egypt's directness and prevent them from exploiting transitions. Can the Prodigy's precision dismantle Warner's rapid counter?

Warner answers with Egypt, a squad known for its incredible work ethic, defensive solidity, and the electrifying pace of its star, Mohamed Salah. Warner's Egyptian assets typically favor a disciplined 4-3-3 or 4-2-3-1, looking to absorb pressure and then break with devastating speed, often funneling play through Salah on the right wing. Players like Arsenal's Mohamed Elneny provide crucial midfield stability, allowing the forwards to focus on their attacking duties. The key tactical question for Warner is whether his disciplined Egyptian unit can contain Barnes's creative Belgian midfielders and prevent them from dictating the tempo. Can Warner's lightning-fast transitions expose Barnes's defense?
