🔬 THE SCOUTING REPORT

Barnes sends out Japan, a squad built on relentless energy, technical precision, and a devastating counter-attack. The Prodigy's tactics often leverage the lightning pace of players like Kaoru Mitoma on the wing, whose dribbling can unlock any defense. Central to Barnes's strategy is also the midfield engine room, where the likes of Wataru Endo's tireless work rate and Takefusa Kubo's creative spark provide both defensive solidity and attacking impetus. The tactical question for Barnes here is how to best exploit Sweden's potential defensive vulnerabilities while maintaining their own disciplined shape against a physically imposing side.

Warner answers with Sweden, a squad renowned for its robust defense, aerial threat, and organized structure. The Young Phenom will be relying on the towering presence of defenders like Victor Lindelöf to marshal the backline and set pieces to be a major offensive weapon, with players such as Alexander Isak offering a sharp point to their attack. Warner’s Sweden typically looks to control the central areas and play a more direct, pragmatic style. The key tactical question for Warner is whether their physical dominance can truly disrupt Japan's intricate passing game and if their forwards can find the clinical edge against a well-drilled Japanese defense.
