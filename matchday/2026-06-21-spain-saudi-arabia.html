🔬 THE SCOUTING REPORT

Warner sends out Spain, a squad renowned for its intricate passing, relentless possession, and a galaxy of midfield maestros. Expect Warner to deploy his assets in a fluid 4-3-3 or 4-2-3-1, with the likes of Rodri anchoring the midfield, providing a platform for creative forces like Pedri and Gavi to dictate tempo and unlock defenses. Up front, players like Morata offer a physical presence, while wingers like Nico Williams provide electrifying pace and directness. Warner's tactical question will be: can his midfield assets maintain enough defensive solidity to prevent quick counter-attacks, while still creating the incisive opportunities needed to break down a resilient opponent? Their ability to convert intricate build-up play into genuine goal-scoring chances will be paramount.

Will answers with Saudi Arabia, a squad that often thrives on defensive discipline, quick transitions, and exploiting space. Will will likely instruct his assets to remain compact, potentially utilizing a 4-5-1 or 4-4-2 shape to stifle Spain's famed midfield. Key players like Salem Al-Dawsari offer flair and danger on the counter, with his ability to drive forward and create moments of magic. The defensive organization, spearheaded by experienced players in the backline, will be crucial. Will's tactical question will be: can his Saudi assets absorb the relentless Spanish pressure for 90 minutes and capitalize on their limited opportunities, turning defensive resilience into offensive thrusts? Their ability to transition quickly from defense to attack, and the clinical finishing of their forwards, will be the deciding factor.
